Correlation analysis was performed with the six sample currencies (refer to main page) to see if there are any correlations among the currency prices. To avoid bias daily return percentage was used instead of absolute price. The result shows (see the Correlation plots below) significant differences between pre and post-spike currencies. The post-spike currencies seem to be strongly co-related with each other suggesting that all currencies reacted to various market forces in a similar pattern and there are statistically significant connections in the way their prices fluctuated. In the other hand the pre-spike currencies do not seem have significant correlation confirming the lack of similar and connected patterns in the cryptocurrency markets.

The darker colors in the below plots refer to weaker correlation and lighter colors refer to stronger correlation. The correlation coefficients can be seen by hovering over the plots.
